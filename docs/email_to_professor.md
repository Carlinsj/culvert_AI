# Email Draft

Subject: Research Project Idea for Predicting Culvert Locations in Ulster County

Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope you are doing well. I am currently working with NYSDOT through the C2SMART summer field
internship, where our team is helping locate and inspect culverts in the field.

One challenge I have noticed in the field is that a significant amount of time is spent finding the
culverts before we can inspect and document them. Once a culvert is found, we already have an app for
entering inspection data, photos, and videos. However, the search process itself can be slow,
especially in rural or heavily vegetated areas.

I wanted to ask if there may be an opportunity next semester for me to work with you and the
hydraulics team on a research project focused on improving culvert location prediction for the
Ulster County side of the Poughkeepsie/Hudson Valley field area. Poughkeepsie itself is in Dutchess
County, so I would scope the pilot to nearby Ulster County communities and corridors west of the
Hudson River, such as Highland/Lloyd, Esopus, New Paltz, Marlboro, Plattekill, Rosendale, and nearby
Kingston approach corridors.

My idea is to use geospatial tools and available datasets such as DEM/topography, road networks,
drainage patterns, Google Earth or aerial imagery, land cover, and areas where culverts are already
registered to train or develop a model that predicts where culverts are likely to be located.

To make the idea more concrete, I have started building a small prototype workflow that scopes the
analysis to the Ulster County pilot area, generates road-stream and road-drainage crossing
candidates, extracts topographic and drainage-related features, and produces a ranked map of likely
culvert locations for field review. Since the main problem is that we often do not know where the
culverts are, the first version would not depend on local culvert inventory labels. Instead, it would
use weak supervision from topography, drainage patterns, road crossings, and Google Earth/manual
imagery review, then improve as field crews confirm locations.

The goal would be to create a tool or workflow that identifies high-probability culvert locations
before field visits. This could help inspection teams reduce search time and complete inspections
more efficiently. A possible first step could be an Ulster County pilot study that compares
predicted locations against verified culvert observations and existing inventory data.

I would be very interested in contributing through field data collection, GIS analysis, model
development, and validation. If you think this idea has potential, I would appreciate the opportunity
to meet with you and discuss how it could be shaped into a research project for next semester.

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
